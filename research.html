Our Research

Our group investigates new molecular mechanisms and compound families for antimicrobial drug discovery. We combine structural biology, enzymology, and biophysics to explore new therapeutic targets.

Current Projects

Multitargeting antimicrobials

The OSA project. Open Source consortium for the Discovery of small-molecule inhibitors towards multiple targets, integrating enzymology, structural biology, and ligand design.

  • ESKAPE pathogens & Tuberculosis
  • Mur ligases
  • X-ray crystallography & high-throughput screening
  • Mechanism-of-action & structure-guided design

Tackling tuberculosis: Reactivating host cell innate immune responses.

Discovery of inhibitors against essential proteins during tuberculosis infection, and reactivating host cell innate immune responses .

  • Essential proteins during infection
  • Cryo-EM / X-ray crystallography
  • Kinetic assays & inhibition profiling
  • Protein degradation & structure-guided design
